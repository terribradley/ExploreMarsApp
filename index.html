Tarot Case Study

Introduction

This project is an app for users to get a Tarot Card Reading. I designed the site to be artful and minimal to keep with the mystical aspect of Tarot.

Conception

I spent time thinking about what Tarot is and what it's like getting a reading from another person. I wanted the site to retain some of the mystery and intution that a reading requires, so I kept instruction to a minimum and tried to design simple call to action buttons that would move a viewer through the site. I wanted to keep the design of the site artful and minimal. I avoided the usual witchy vibe that comes with Tarot. I want the site to be appreciated by people who may not know much about Tarot, so the design is kept tasteful. The logo and drawings are clean. The call to action buttons are unique, yet simple. The color pallet is fairly neutral, so that there is no distraction from the epic images on the cards. I made everything for this app; the logo, icons, and card images, and card backs.

Process

I played around with the layout and organization of the app many times. It was a challenge helping viewers navigate the site without instructions presented in words. There is a form field that a viewer can type a question or concern. Upon submitting, the screen scrolls down for them.
